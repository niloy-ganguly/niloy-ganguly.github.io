Finding clusters and study of their properties obtained from ImmuneSearch technique

 

ImmuneSearch algorithm draws its basic inspiration from simple and well known concept of the human immune system where cells undergo mutation and opportunistic proliferation to generate antibodies which track the antigens (foreign objects). It is implemented independently by each individual peer participating in the network and is totally decentralized in nature. The algorithm uses affinity-governed proliferation and mutation to spread query message packets (antibodies) across the network. It also evolves the topology of the p2p network in terms of adjusting the neighborhood of the participating peers (information in peers - antigens) based upon some criteria ex: proximity with the queried item, similarity metric. Initial connections between the peers are represented as a 2-dimensional regular Moore’s grid topology. The topology evolution of the network results into an arrangement whereby similar contents are grouped together i.e. clusters are formed.

           

The project should focus on the study of the following:

 

-         Finding clusters using standard cluster finding algorithms.

-         Analyzing the graph properties of the clusters formed.

-         Perform experiment considering some other initial topology of the peers ex: power law, E-R graph .

-         Propose or suggest some other rule for topology evolution that may produce better search performance after several generations.

 

Reference:

 

-         Niloy Ganguly, Geoff Canright, Andreas Deutsch, “Design of a Robust Search Algorithm for P2P Networks”, HIPC 2004.

-         Niloy Ganguly, Geoff Canright, Andreas Deutsch, “Design Of An Efficient Search Algorithm For P2P Networks Using Concepts From Natural Immune Systems”, PPSN 2004.
