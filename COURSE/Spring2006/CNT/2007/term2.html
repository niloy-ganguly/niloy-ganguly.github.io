Complex networks in biology: enzymes - metabolic pathways network:

Description: Enzymes are proteins that catalyze (i.e. accelerate) chemical reactions. In these reactions, the molecules at the beginning of the process are called substrates, and the enzyme converts them into different molecules, the products. Almost all processes in the cell need enzymes in order to occur at significant rates. A metabolic pathway is a series of chemical reactions occurring within a cell, catalyzed by enzymes, resulting in either the formation of a metabolic product to be used or stored by the cell, or the initiation of another metabolic pathway. Since enzymes are extremely selective for their substrates and speed up only a few reactions from among many possibilities, the set of enzymes made in a cell determines which metabolic pathways occur in that cell.

Abstract: This network consists of all enzymes who take part in 70 metabolic pathways will be the nodes of the network. If two particular enzymes take part in same metabolic pathway then there will be link between them. The weight of the link is equal to the number of metabolic pathways they both participate. Different organisms will pose different networks. We will take some of these networks, analyze those individually and correlate them. We will try to find some evidence of evolution among the considered organisms.

There are other ways to represent the same data. We will construct bipartite networks from same data where bottom nodes will be the enzymes and top nodes will be the metabolic pathways. Assuming the bottom set of nodes is constant, we will analyze the different properties (i.e. degree distribution) of bottom set of nodes and their changing pattern among different organisms. And will try to find some evidence of evolution.
