Complex networks in biology: protein – protein interaction network:

Description: protein is a large complex molecule made up of one or more chains of amino acids. Proteins perform a wide variety of activities in the cell. Virtually every cellular process is regulated by protein-protein interaction. These interactions are operative at almost every level of cell function. Protein-protein interactions fall into three functional categories: metabolic and signaling pathways; morphogenic pathways in which groups of proteins participate in the same cellular function during a developmental process; and structural complexes and molecular machines in which numerous macromolecules are brought together.

Abstract: This network is viewed as a graph whose nodes correspond to proteins. Two proteins are connected by an edge if they interact. Available data is huge. We can take a few organism (i.e. escherichia coli, yeast, fruit fly, human) as our model. We will try to analyze the network and find its various properties from complex network viewpoint. We will also try to speculate the roles of different nodes (proteins) in its real cellular system. For example highly connected proteins are more important than lower one.
