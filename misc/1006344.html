Tenth Plan to emphasise on convergence

TIMES NEWS NETWORK[ FRIDAY, FEBRUARY 15, 2002 01:31:21 AM ]
kolkata: it might converge as music to the ears of the infotech brigade. the government of india will lay heavy emphasis on convergence and e-governance during the tenth five-year plan. this was disclosed by santosh rungta, vice-president of the bharat chamber of commerce while delivering the welcome address at an interactive session on convergent technology and knowledge management held in the city on thursday. "i am confident that with the implementation of various programmes for the tenth plan, a new era would emerge in convergence which will not only enhance the image of the country in the international scenario but also create several job opportunities. as businessmen, therefore, we should whole-heartedly support this development and participate in the programme by timely adoption of technologies that would be available to us in the near future," said rungta. elaborating on technological changes and deregulation processes that are taking place throughout the region, rungta maintained that changes in markets have led to the convergence of ownership and services across national boundaries, creating gaps and contradictions in india's national policies. "driven largely by technological developments, which can be a boon or a bane for developing countries, the converging communication environment has profound policy implications. visions developed for the future foresee the eventual emergence of an information society in india based on convergence of telecom, broadcasting and computers," rungta added. other speakers at the forum included roopen roy, executive director, pricewaterhouse coopers, niloy ganguly, faculty member, indian institute of social welfare and business management sourav sarkar, ceo, aesthetic technologies. in his address, roy, echoing rungta's views, said that there was tremendous opportunity for india to create wealth from the convergence revolution. "convergence, which implies bringing together of two goddesses, laxmi and saraswati, can create wonders," he said.
